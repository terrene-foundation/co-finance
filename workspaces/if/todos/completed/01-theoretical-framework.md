## Description

Create a clear, accessible explanation of the theoretical framework that underpins Discussion Theme 1. This is the foundation everything else builds on — students need to understand the theory before analyzing current events through its lens.

## Deliverable

A document in `workspaces/if/01-analysis/03-deep-dive/01-theoretical-framework.md` covering:

1. **The Impossible Trinity (Mundell-Fleming Trilemma)**
   - What it is: a country cannot simultaneously have all three of (1) free capital flows, (2) fixed exchange rate, (3) independent monetary policy
   - Why it matters: forces every country to choose which two to prioritize
   - Visual diagram showing the triangle with the three corners

2. **The Oil-Trade-Monetary Policy Triangle (our adaptation)**
   - How the current crisis creates a _parallel_ trilemma for policymakers:
     - Oil shock (inflation pressure from supply side)
     - Trade costs (tariffs adding to consumer prices)
     - Monetary policy response (cut rates for growth vs. hold/raise for inflation)
   - Why you can't solve all three at once — fighting inflation with rate hikes slows growth; cutting rates for growth worsens inflation; removing tariffs helps inflation but undermines trade policy goals

3. **Key Concepts Students Need**
   - Supply-side vs demand-side inflation
   - Stagflation — what it is, why it's so hard for policymakers
   - Exchange rate pass-through (how oil/tariff costs flow into domestic prices)
   - Terms of trade shock

4. **Real-world analogies** to make the theory stick (per pedagogy rules)
