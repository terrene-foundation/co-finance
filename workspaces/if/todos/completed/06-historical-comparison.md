# Todo: Historical Comparison — How Does 2026 Compare to Past Crises?

## Status: pending

## Description

Compare the current Oil-Trade-Monetary Policy Triangle to historical precedents. Students benefit from seeing patterns across time — it helps them understand what's genuinely new vs. what's a recurring dynamic in international finance.

## Deliverable

A document in `workspaces/if/01-analysis/03-deep-dive/06-historical-comparison.md` covering:

1. **1973 Oil Embargo (OPEC)**
   - Similarities: Geopolitical conflict → oil supply disruption → inflation → central bank dilemma
   - Differences: 1973 was a deliberate embargo by producers; 2026 is collateral damage from military conflict. No tariff layer in 1973. Dollar was newly off the gold standard (Bretton Woods collapsed 1971)
   - Outcome: Stagflation, recession, end of the postwar boom

2. **1979 Iranian Revolution / Second Oil Shock**
   - Similarities: Iran at the center; supply disruption through Hormuz region; Volcker's rate hike dilemma mirrors today's central bank bind
   - Differences: Volcker chose to crush inflation (20% fed funds rate). Will today's Fed make the same choice at 3.75%?
   - Outcome: Deep recession (1981-82) but inflation was broken

3. **1985 Plaza Accord**
   - Relevance: Coordinated dollar depreciation by G5 to address trade imbalances — the last time major economies cooperated on exchange rates
   - Contrast: Today's dollar weakness is uncoordinated and driven by tariff retaliation, not cooperation
   - Question: Is multilateral coordination still possible in a fragmented world?

4. **2008 Oil Price Spike + Financial Crisis**
   - Similarities: Oil hit $147/barrel in July 2008, contributing to recession
   - Differences: 2008 was primarily a financial/credit crisis; 2026 is geopolitical + trade + energy
   - Lesson: Oil shocks interact with existing vulnerabilities to amplify crises

5. **Smoot-Hawley Tariff Act (1930)**
   - Relevance: Last time US tariffs were at comparable levels (~13% effective rate now vs. ~20% then)
   - Lesson: Smoot-Hawley deepened the Great Depression by triggering global retaliation. History rhyming?
   - Difference: 1930 had no central banks with modern policy tools; today's institutions are different

6. **What's Genuinely New About 2026**
   - Speed: Three legal tariff frameworks in three weeks; oil shock in days, not months
   - Interconnection: Financial markets are more globally connected than in any prior crisis
   - BRICS/multipolar factor: Alternative financial infrastructure didn't exist in prior crises
   - CBDCs and digital finance: New transmission channels that didn't exist before
