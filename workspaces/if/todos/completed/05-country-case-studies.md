# Todo: Country Case Studies — The Triangle in Different Economies

## Status: pending

## Description

Pick 4-5 countries that illustrate different positions in the Oil-Trade-Monetary Policy Triangle and write short case studies showing how the same global events create very different pressures depending on a country's structure. This makes the theory concrete and gives students material for group work or debate.

## Deliverable

A document in `workspaces/if/01-analysis/03-deep-dive/05-country-case-studies.md` covering:

1. **Japan** — The Most Exposed
   - 90% of oil from Middle East, 70% through Hormuz
   - BOJ in first hiking cycle in decades (0.75%)
   - Yen as safe haven vs. energy import cost pressure
   - Export economy facing tariffs AND higher input costs
   - KOSPI-like crash risk in Nikkei

2. **United States** — The Conflicted Hegemon
   - World's largest oil producer AND consumer
   - Imposing tariffs while fighting tariff-driven inflation
   - Fed caught between dual mandate signals
   - Dollar weakening despite tariffs and safe-haven status
   - Owns the reserve currency — both a privilege and a constraint

3. **India** — The Rising Power Navigating Both Sides
   - Major oil importer (vulnerable to shock)
   - Negotiating trade deal with US ($500B framework)
   - RBI paused easing at 5.25%
   - BRICS member but US trade partner — balancing act
   - "Russian crude penalty" tariff removed — US leveraging India's energy sourcing

4. **Germany/Eurozone** — The Fiscal Rule-Breaker
   - Energy vulnerable (Iran war hits hard)
   - Broke constitutional debt brake for defense spending
   - ECB holding at 2.00% but euro appreciation complicating things
   - EU-US trade deal in flux
   - Germany as bellwether for European fiscal architecture change

5. **South Korea** — The Canary in the Coal Mine
   - KOSPI crashed 18% in two days — worst since 2008
   - Heavily energy-dependent, export-oriented
   - Won at weakest since 2009
   - Section 301 target for US trade investigation
   - Why South Korea's market was the most volatile globally

## Acceptance Criteria

- Each case study clearly shows how the three forces (oil, trade, monetary policy) interact differently based on the country's economic structure
- Students should be able to compare cases and identify patterns
- Each case includes key data points (not just narrative)
