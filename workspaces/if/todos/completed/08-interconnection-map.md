## Status: pending

## Description

Create a visual/narrative map showing how the three arms of the triangle (oil, trade, monetary policy) connect to each other and to broader events (market selloff, gold surge, capital flows, BRICS). This is the "big picture" document that shows students the system, not just individual events.

## Deliverable

A document in `workspaces/if/01-analysis/03-deep-dive/08-interconnection-map.md` covering:

1. **The Causal Web** (narrative + diagram description)
   - Iran war → oil surge → inflation pressure → central bank holds/raises → dollar dynamics → EM capital flows → market selloff
   - IEEPA ruling → tariff uncertainty → Section 122 → trade cost inflation → consumer squeeze → overlaps with oil inflation
   - Both channels → stagflation risk → central bank paralysis → market volatility → safe-haven rotation (gold, franc, yen)
   - Feedback loops: market selloff → wealth effect → lower growth → makes central bank cut case stronger → but inflation still high

2. **Reinforcing vs. Offsetting Effects**
   - Reinforcing: Oil inflation + tariff inflation = double hit to consumers
   - Reinforcing: Dollar weakness + oil prices (dollar-denominated) = even higher import costs for non-US countries
   - Offsetting: Dollar weakness helps US exporters but hurts import costs
   - Offsetting: Gold surge helps gold-producing nations but signals systemic fear
   - Offsetting: BRICS local currency settlement reduces dollar dependence but is too small to change macro dynamics yet

3. **Second-Order Effects Students Might Miss**
   - Trade finance disruption: Uncertain tariff rates make letters of credit harder to price
   - Insurance market stress: Maritime insurers withdrawing from Hormuz AND from tariff-related trade credit
   - Supply chain inventory hoarding: Businesses stockpiling before tariff deadlines, adding to inflationary pressure
   - Fiscal pressure: Governments spending more on defense (Germany) and energy subsidies while tax revenues uncertain

4. **The Key Question for Discussion**
   - "If you could change ONE variable in this system, which would have the biggest positive cascading effect?" (Ending the Iran war? Removing tariffs? Central banks coordinating?)
