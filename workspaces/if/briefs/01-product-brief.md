# Product Brief

## Product

A curated research digest of interesting events and trends in international finance from the past two weeks (late February – mid March 2026). The output is a discussion-ready document for an International Finance class, with key events, context, and discussion prompts to help students engage with real-world developments.

## Objectives

- Identify 8-12 significant international finance events/trends from the past 2 weeks
- Provide context and background for each event so students can understand the significance
- Include discussion questions that connect events to international finance concepts
- Cover diverse topics: exchange rates, trade policy, central bank actions, capital flows, sovereign debt, emerging markets, geopolitical finance

## Tech Stack

- Backend: N/A — this is a research/analysis output, not a software product
- Frontend: N/A
- Database: N/A
- AI: Claude for research synthesis

## Constraints

- Events must be from approximately Feb 26 – Mar 12, 2026
- Content must be factual and sourced
- Should connect to international finance theory and concepts studied in class
- Educational purpose — not investment advice

## Users

- Students in an International Finance class
- Instructor leading class discussions
