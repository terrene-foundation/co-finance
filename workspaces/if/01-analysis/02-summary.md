# International Finance: Events & Trends Digest

## Late February – Mid March 2026

### Overview

The past two weeks have been among the most eventful in international finance in decades. Two seismic events — a US-Israel war on Iran and a Supreme Court ruling striking down presidential tariff authority — are reshaping energy markets, trade policy, capital flows, and central bank decision-making simultaneously. Below is a curated list of 12 events/trends organized by theme, each with discussion questions connecting to international finance concepts.

---

## The Big Picture: Everything Is Connected

These events don't exist in isolation. The Iran war drives oil prices up, which creates inflation, which constrains central banks from cutting rates, which strengthens the dollar's safe-haven appeal (short-term) while weakening it structurally (long-term), which affects emerging market capital flows, which interacts with the tariff uncertainty from the Supreme Court ruling, which is reshaping trade relationships, which drives China's export diversion to ASEAN, which... and so on.

**The best class discussions will trace these interconnections.**

## Events by Theme

### I. Geopolitics & Energy (Events 1, 12)

1. **Iran War & Strait of Hormuz Crisis** — Oil surged ~55% (from ~$71 to $110+/barrel, peaking at $120) as the strait (20% of global oil) was effectively shut down. Asian economies (Japan, South Korea) hit hardest.
2. **Global Sanctions Escalation** — Simultaneous regimes against Russia, Iran, Venezuela, Sudan, Rwanda creating compliance complexity for banks and shippers.

### II. Trade Policy Revolution (Events 2, 6, 10)

3. **Supreme Court Strikes Down IEEPA Tariffs** — Most consequential trade law ruling in decades. $142-175B in refunds pending. Administration pivoting through Section 122 (150-day cap) and Section 301 investigations.
4. **China's Record Trade Surplus** — Exports up 21.8% despite US tariffs. Trade diversion to ASEAN (+29.4%), Europe (+27.8%). US exports to China down 26%.
5. **Trade Deals in Flux** — EU-US deal frozen then thawing; US-India $500B framework; Canada-China rapprochement; WTO MC14 approaching March 26.

### III. Financial Markets (Events 3, 4)

6. **Global Market Selloff** — South Korea KOSPI crashed 18% in two days (worst since 2008). Asian markets disproportionately affected. Partial recovery on de-escalation signals.
7. **Gold Past $5,000/oz** — Approximately doubled from 2024. Driven by safe-haven demand, central bank purchases (de-dollarization), and weakening dollar.

### IV. Monetary Policy (Event 5)

8. **Central Bank "Super Week"** (March 16-19) — Seven major central banks decide in 3 days. The stagflation dilemma: oil inflation vs. growth fears. Bank of England swung from "expected to cut" to "70% chance of hike." Turkey at 37%, Switzerland at 0% — extraordinary global divergence.

### V. Structural Shifts (Events 7, 8, 9, 11)

9. **Germany Breaks Its Fiscal Rules** — Defense spending to 2.8% of GDP; constitutional debt brake exempted. Reshapes European fiscal architecture and Bund market.
10. **Record Sovereign Debt** — $61T OECD, $12.1T emerging markets. 80% of 2026 issuance is refinancing, not new spending. 40% of African countries in debt distress.
11. **EM Capital Flows & Latin America** — EM flows collapsed from $100B (January) to $22B (February). Yet Latin American equities rallied 20% to eleven-year high. Africa projected to outgrow Asia (4.6% vs 4.1%).
12. **Digital Currencies & BRICS** — China paying interest on digital yuan wallets. BRICS now 11 members, trade flows expected to exceed G7. Dollar weakening 9.4% in 2025. De-dollarization debate intensifying.

## Top Discussion Themes for Class

### 1. The Oil-Trade-Monetary Policy Triangle

The Iran war (oil shock) + tariff upheaval (trade costs) + central bank dilemma (cut or hold?) form a three-way tension that illustrates the "impossible trinity" and policy trade-offs in open economies.

### 2. Separation of Powers and Trade Policy Credibility

The IEEPA ruling shows how domestic institutional constraints (Constitution, courts) affect international trade credibility. When the legal basis for tariffs can be struck down overnight, how do trading partners plan?

### 3. Trade Diversion vs. Trade Destruction

China's record surplus despite US tariffs is a textbook case of trade diversion. Goods don't disappear — they find new buyers. What does this mean for the effectiveness of unilateral tariffs?

### 4. Safe Havens and the Dollar Paradox

Gold at $5,000, Swiss franc surging, yen catching safe-haven bids — yet the dollar is weakening structurally. Tariffs were supposed to strengthen the dollar; retaliation did the opposite. What determines safe-haven status?

### 5. Sovereign Debt Sustainability

$61 trillion in OECD sovereign bonds, 80% of new issuance just for refinancing, and now higher defense spending (Germany), energy subsidies (everyone), and lower growth (oil shock). When does the math stop working?

### 6. The Multipolar Financial System

BRICS trade exceeding G7, digital yuan paying interest, local currency settlement at 90% within BRICS. Is de-dollarization happening or still theoretical? What would the transition look like?
