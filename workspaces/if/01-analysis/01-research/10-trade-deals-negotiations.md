# Event 10: International Trade Deals and Negotiations

## US-Canada Trade War and USMCA

- **March 4**: US tariffs on Canadian goods take effect; Canada retaliates simultaneously
- **March 5-6**: Trump exempts auto imports and USMCA-compliant goods (~38% of Canadian imports) until April 2
- **Significance**: North American auto manufacturing is deeply integrated across all three USMCA countries — tariffing compliant goods undermines the very agreement the US negotiated

## EU-US Trade Deal

- **Status**: Deal negotiated in summer 2025 (15% ceiling on most EU products) was frozen after Section 122 tariffs created uncertainty
- **March 10**: European Parliament signals readiness to vote — committee vote March 19, plenary March 26
- **Deal features**: Sunset clause (expires March 2028), suspension clause if US violates terms — reflecting deep EU distrust of US trade commitments
- **Significance**: Affects ~$1 trillion in annual bilateral trade

## US-India Trade Framework

- **Status**: "Close to the finish line" per officials
- **Key terms**: India's reciprocal tariff reduced from 25% to 18%; India commits to purchasing $500 billion in US products over 5 years (energy, aircraft, tech, coal)
- **Significance**: The $500 billion procurement is massive; removal of "Russian crude penalty" tariff reflects US leverage over India's energy sourcing

## Canada-China Tariff Reductions

- **March 1**: China reduces tariffs on Canadian canola (from 84% to ~15%), removes tariffs on lobster, crab, peas
- **Significance**: Strategically timed as Canada's US trade relationship deteriorates — China positioning itself as an alternative partner

## WTO 14th Ministerial Conference

- **Scheduled**: March 26-29, 2026 in Yaounde, Cameroon
- **Context**: Rules-based trading system under maximum stress — US shifted tariff legal strategies three times in weeks
- **Significance**: Tests whether the WTO can remain relevant in an era of aggressive unilateralism

## Discussion Angles

- How do bilateral deals (US-India, US-EU) interact with the multilateral WTO system? Do they strengthen or undermine it?
- What does China's strategic tariff reduction on Canadian goods tell us about how trade policy is used as a geopolitical tool?
- Is the USMCA being undermined by the very country that negotiated it? What does that mean for trade agreement credibility?
- Can the WTO survive when its largest member is operating outside its rules?
