# Event 3: Global Stock Market Selloff

## Summary

The Iran war triggered a severe selloff across global equity markets in the first week of March, with Asian markets hit hardest due to energy import dependence. South Korea's KOSPI suffered its worst two-day crash since 2008, and circuit breakers were activated.

## Key Market Moves (March 3-9)

| Market            | Move              | Notes                                             |
| ----------------- | ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| South Korea KOSPI | -18% (two days)   | Worst since 2008; circuit breaker activated       |
| Japan Nikkei 225  | -6.45% (Mar 9)    | Entered correction territory (10%+ from Feb high) |
| Taiwan TAIEX      | -4.86%            |                                                   |
| Vietnam VN-Index  | -5.7%             |                                                   |
| India BSE SENSEX  | -9.0% YTD         | Worst-performing major index globally             |
| US S&P 500        | -3% from Jan high | Dow and Nasdaq down 5%+ from peaks                |

Markets partially recovered on March 10 after Trump signaled de-escalation.

## Countries/Regions Affected

Asia-Pacific (South Korea, Japan, Taiwan, Vietnam, India) most severely; US and Europe also affected but less dramatically.

## Financial Significance

- **Asian vulnerability exposed**: Energy-dependent export economies are extremely sensitive to Middle East supply disruptions
- **Circuit breaker activation**: A rare event signaling panic-level selling, not orderly price discovery
- **Fragile recovery**: March 10 bounce driven by diplomatic signals, not fundamental resolution
- **Capital flight**: Money flowing from Asian equities to safe havens (gold, US Treasuries, Swiss franc)

## Discussion Angles

- Why did Asian markets fall so much harder than US/European markets?
- What role do circuit breakers play in market stability — do they prevent panic or amplify it?
- How does the "contagion" of a regional crisis spread through interconnected global capital markets?
- Compare the speed of this selloff to historical precedents (2008, COVID-2020)
