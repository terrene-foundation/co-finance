# Event 9: Emerging Market Capital Flows and Latin America Rally

## Summary

Portfolio flows to emerging markets slowed dramatically to $21.7 billion in February 2026 (down from a record $100.5 billion in January). Meanwhile, Latin American equities rallied over 20%, reaching an eleven-year high on the MSCI EM Latin America Index.

## Capital Flows

- **February EM inflows**: $21.7 billion (vs $100.5 billion in January, $45.5 billion in Feb 2025)
- **Breakdown**: Debt inflows $14.3 billion, equity inflows $7.4 billion
- **Regional**: Asia $5.9 billion debt, Latin America $4.3 billion, emerging Europe $2.6 billion
- **Note**: This data predates the Iran war — March flows expected to deteriorate further

## Latin America Rally

- **MSCI EM Latin America Index**: +20% in 2026, eleven-year high
- **Drivers**: Global rotation from overvalued US tech to commodity exporters; strong foreign inflows
- **Peru**: Leads regional growth on record gold and copper prices
- **Risks**: Brazil and Mexico fiscal deficits above historical averages; vulnerable to oil shock

## Sub-Saharan Africa Growth

- **IMF projects**: 4.6% average growth in 2026, exceeding Asia's 4.1% for the first time
- **East Africa**: Leads at 5.8% (Ethiopia, Kenya)
- **Headwinds**: 40% of countries in debt distress; US foreign aid cuts ($1.1 billion loss projected for Ethiopia alone)

## Discussion Angles

- Why did EM flows collapse from $100 billion to $22 billion in one month? What drives capital flow volatility?
- How does the "risk-on / risk-off" dynamic affect emerging markets?
- Why are Latin American equities rallying while broader EM flows are slowing — what explains the divergence?
- Is sub-Saharan Africa overtaking Asia in growth a milestone or misleading statistic (given the different base levels)?
- How do US aid cuts affect the financial stability of aid-dependent economies?
