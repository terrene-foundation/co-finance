# Event 4: Gold Surges Past $5,000/oz

## Summary

Gold prices hit all-time highs, breaching $5,000/oz for the first time in January 2026 and reaching ~$5,400-5,500/oz by early March. The rally accelerated after the Iran war began. Analysts at Deutsche Bank and Societe Generale forecast $6,000/oz by year-end.

## Key Drivers

1. **Safe-haven demand**: Iran war and geopolitical uncertainty
2. **Weakening US dollar**: DXY down 9.4% in 2025, continued decline in early 2026
3. **Central bank purchases**: China, India, Russia actively diversifying reserves away from dollars
4. **Fed rate cut expectations**: Lower rates reduce the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding gold

## Countries/Regions Affected

Global — particular buying from non-Western central banks; benefits gold-producing nations (Australia, South Africa, Peru, Canada).

## Financial Significance

- **Approximately doubled from 2024 levels** — extraordinary appreciation
- **Central bank reserve diversification**: A structural, long-term driver beyond the current crisis
- **De-dollarization signal**: Central banks buying gold instead of US Treasuries reflects shifting confidence in dollar-denominated reserves
- **Wealth effect**: Benefits commodity-exporting economies; raises production costs for industries using gold (electronics, jewelry)

## Discussion Angles

- Is gold still an effective safe haven in a world of CBDCs and crypto?
- What does central bank gold buying signal about the future of the dollar-based reserve system?
- How does gold's surge relate to the "Triffin dilemma" — the tension between domestic monetary policy and the dollar's reserve currency role?
- Compare gold's performance to other safe havens (US Treasuries, Swiss franc, Japanese yen) during this crisis
