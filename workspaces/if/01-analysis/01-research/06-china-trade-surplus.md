# Event 6: China's Record Trade Surplus and Export Surge

## Summary

China's January-February 2026 trade data (released March 10) showed exports rising 21.8% year-on-year (vs. 7.1% expected), producing a combined surplus of $213.62 billion — the highest on record for the period. Semiconductor exports alone surged 66.5%.

## Key Data Points

- **Exports**: +21.8% YoY (more than triple expectations)
- **Semiconductor exports**: +66.5% (fastest growth in a decade)
- **Trade surplus**: $213.62 billion for Jan-Feb combined (record)
- **Export destinations**: ASEAN +29.4%, Europe +27.8%, South Korea +27%
- **US exports to China**: Dropped 26% from 2024 levels
- **Full-year trajectory**: On track to exceed 2025's record $1.2 trillion surplus

## Countries/Regions Affected

China, ASEAN, EU, South Korea (as new export destinations); United States (declining trade relationship with China).

## Financial Significance

- **Trade diversion in action**: Despite US tariffs at ~30% effective rate on Chinese goods, China successfully redirected exports to alternative markets rather than reducing production
- **Limits of unilateral tariffs**: One country's tariffs don't eliminate trade — they reroute it
- **China "stopped buying American"**: US exports to China down 26% suggests retaliatory behavior beyond formal tariffs
- **Yuan implications**: $1.2 trillion+ surplus creates pressure for yuan appreciation
- **Supply chain resilience**: China's manufacturing ecosystem proved more adaptable than critics expected

## Discussion Angles

- Does this prove the theory of "trade diversion" vs "trade destruction"? What determines which effect dominates?
- How can the US achieve its stated trade deficit reduction goals if tariffed goods simply flow to other markets?
- What are the implications for ASEAN countries absorbing redirected Chinese exports — do they benefit or face competitive pressure?
- How does China's semiconductor export surge relate to US tech export controls? Is China becoming self-sufficient faster than expected?
