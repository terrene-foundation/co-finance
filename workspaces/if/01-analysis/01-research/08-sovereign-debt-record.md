# Event 8: Record Global Sovereign Debt Levels

## Summary

Per the OECD Global Debt Report 2026 (released early March), central government borrowing hit $17 trillion in 2025 with outstanding sovereign bonds reaching a record $61 trillion (up from $55 trillion in 2024). EM sovereign bond issuance reached $3.4 trillion, bringing EM total debt to $12.1 trillion. About 40% of African countries are in debt distress or at high risk.

## Key Data Points

- **OECD sovereign bonds outstanding**: $61 trillion (record)
- **2026 issuance purpose**: 80% goes to refinancing maturing debt, not new spending
- **EM sovereign debt stock**: $12.1 trillion
- **Borrowing increase**: Governments and corporations expected to borrow 17% more from bond markets in 2026 vs 2024
- **Maturity shift**: Governments moving to shorter-term issuance because long-term borrowing costs have risen
- **African debt distress**: ~40% of countries in distress or at high risk

## Financial Significance

- **Refinancing risk**: Shorter maturities mean more frequent rollovers at potentially higher rates
- **Fiscal crowding out**: Rising interest costs squeeze budgets for other spending
- **Vulnerability to shocks**: Dependence on continuous market access makes sovereigns fragile during disruptions like the Iran crisis
- **EM pressure**: Higher-for-longer rates in developed markets pull capital away from emerging markets

## Discussion Angles

- What is "fiscal sustainability" and how do we measure it? When does sovereign debt become "too much"?
- How does the shift to shorter-term maturities create a "doom loop" risk?
- Compare debt-to-GDP dynamics across developed vs. emerging economies
- What role do international institutions (IMF, World Bank) play when 40% of African nations face debt distress?
- How does the Iran crisis exacerbate sovereign debt pressures (higher defense spending, energy subsidies, lower growth)?
