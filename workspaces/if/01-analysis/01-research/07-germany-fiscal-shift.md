# Event 7: Germany's Historic Fiscal and Defense Expansion

## Summary

Germany is undertaking its largest military buildup in decades, with defense spending rising to ~83 billion euros in 2026 (up 20 billion from 2025), reaching 2.8% of GDP and targeting 3.5% by 2029. Chancellor Merz won parliamentary approval to exempt defense spending above 1% of GDP from Germany's constitutional debt brake ("Schuldenbremse"), breaking decades of fiscal conservatism.

## Financial Significance

- **Constitutional shift**: Germany abandoning balanced-budget doctrine is a fundamental change in European fiscal architecture
- **GDP impact**: Goldman Sachs estimates this fiscal shift contributes nearly half of Germany's projected 1.1% GDP growth in 2026
- **Bond market implications**: German Bund issuance will increase substantially, potentially raising yields and reshaping the euro-area interest rate landscape
- **Defense sector**: Benefits European defense contractors (Rheinmetall, KNDS, Airbus Defence)
- **EU fiscal spillover**: If Germany loosens fiscal policy, pressure mounts on other EU members to follow

## Discussion Angles

- How does Germany's fiscal expansion affect the euro and eurozone interest rates?
- What happens to the European fiscal architecture when its most disciplined member breaks ranks?
- Compare the economic multiplier effects of defense spending vs. infrastructure or social spending
- How does this relate to the broader geopolitical context (Ukraine, NATO 2% commitment, European strategic autonomy)?
