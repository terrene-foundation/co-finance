# Academic Writing

A practical guide to writing well-structured academic papers in finance. Covers the standard thesis/dissertation structure, how to construct rigorous arguments, how to write literature reviews that synthesize rather than summarize, and how to describe research methodology clearly. Aimed at UG/PG students writing research papers, honours theses, or dissertations in finance and economics.

## Quick Reference

1. **Thesis Structure**: Introduction --> Literature Review --> Methodology --> Results --> Discussion --> Conclusion
2. **Claim-Evidence-Warrant**: Every argument needs a claim (what you assert), evidence (data/citations supporting it), and a warrant (why the evidence supports the claim)
3. **Literature Review Purpose**: Identify what is known, what is debated, and what gaps remain -- then position your research in that gap
4. **Strong Research Question**: Specific, researchable, significant, and grounded in existing literature
5. **Methodology Section**: Must be detailed enough that another researcher could replicate your study
