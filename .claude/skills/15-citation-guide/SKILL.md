# Citation Guide

A practical reference for the three citation styles most commonly used in finance and economics research -- APA 7th edition, Chicago/Turabian, and Harvard. Includes templates and examples for the source types finance students cite most frequently: journal articles, textbooks, working papers, data sources, and financial reports. Also covers the most common citation mistakes and how to avoid them.

## Quick Reference

1. **APA 7th** -- Author-date system; dominant in social sciences and business schools. Format: (Author, Year)
2. **Chicago/Turabian** -- Two systems: notes-bibliography (footnotes) for humanities, author-date for sciences. Common in economics journals
3. **Harvard** -- Author-date system similar to APA; widely used in UK/Australian universities
4. **DOI Preference** -- Always include a DOI (Digital Object Identifier) when available; it is a permanent link to the source
5. **Data Citation** -- Financial databases (Bloomberg, CRSP, FRED) need proper citation including retrieval date and access details
