# FNCE101 Foundations

The essential building blocks of finance -- the concepts and tools that every subsequent finance course assumes you know. Covers time value of money (the single most important concept in finance), how to value stocks and bonds, how to read and analyze financial statements, and the fundamental relationship between risk and return. Designed for first-year undergraduate finance students or anyone building a solid foundation.

## Quick Reference

1. **Present Value**: PV = FV / (1 + r)^n -- a dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow
2. **CAPM**: E(r_i) = r_f + beta_i x [E(r_m) - r_f] -- expected return depends on systematic risk
3. **Gordon Growth Model**: P_0 = D_1 / (r - g) -- stock price as a perpetuity of growing dividends
4. **Bond Price**: PV of coupon annuity + PV of face value at maturity
5. **Sharpe Ratio**: [E(r_p) - r_f] / sigma_p -- reward per unit of total risk
