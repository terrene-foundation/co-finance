# Corporate Finance

Core corporate finance topics for undergraduate and postgraduate finance students. Covers how firms make financing decisions (capital structure), evaluate investments (capital budgeting), return value to shareholders (dividend policy), and manage short-term liquidity (working capital). Essential for anyone pursuing corporate finance, investment banking, or financial management roles.

## Quick Reference

1. **WACC** = (E/V) x r_e + (D/V) x r_d x (1 - T) -- the hurdle rate for investment decisions
2. **NPV Rule** -- accept projects where NPV > 0; the gold standard of capital budgeting
3. **Modigliani-Miller Proposition I (with taxes)** -- V_L = V_U + T x D; debt creates value through tax shields
4. **Cash Conversion Cycle** = DIO + DSO - DPO; measures how long cash is tied up in operations
5. **IRR** -- the discount rate that makes NPV = 0; compare to WACC to decide on projects
