# Case Analysis Method

The Harvard case method: how to systematically analyze a business or finance case study.

## The Three Phases

### Phase 1: Read

- Read the case once quickly for the overall picture
- Read again carefully, highlighting key facts, data, and quotes
- Identify the protagonist (who is making the decision?) and the decision to be made
- Note the timeline and any deadlines

### Phase 2: Analyze

1. **Situation analysis**: What is the current state? Use financial statements, market data, and qualitative information from the case.
2. **Problem identification**: What is the core problem or decision? (Often different from the symptoms)
3. **Root cause analysis**: Why does this problem exist? Look for structural, financial, or strategic causes.
4. **Generate alternatives**: List 3-4 realistic options (including "do nothing")
5. **Evaluate alternatives**: Assess each option against criteria (financial impact, risk, feasibility, strategic fit)
6. **Recommend**: Choose the best option and justify your choice

### Phase 3: Discuss (or Write Up)

Present your analysis clearly with evidence from the case. Anticipate counterarguments.

## Situation Analysis Framework

| Dimension | Questions to Ask |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Financial health** | What do the financial statements tell us? Revenue trend? Margins? Leverage? Cash flow? |
| **Industry position** | What is the competitive landscape? Market share? Growth rate? |
| **Internal capabilities** | What are the firm's strengths and weaknesses? Management quality? |
| **External environment** | What macro factors affect the decision? Regulation? Technology? Economy? |
| **Stakeholders** | Who is affected? Shareholders, employees, customers, regulators? |

## Decision Criteria

When evaluating alternatives, use explicit criteria:

- **Financial impact**: NPV, IRR, payback, EPS accretion/dilution
- **Strategic fit**: Does it align with the firm's strategy?
- **Risk**: What could go wrong? How likely? How severe?
- **Feasibility**: Can the firm actually execute this option? (Resources, capabilities, timeline)
- **Stakeholder impact**: How do different stakeholders benefit or suffer?

## Common Mistakes

1. **Retelling the case**: Your analysis should add value beyond what is written. The professor knows what the case says.
2. **Not taking a position**: "It depends" is not a recommendation. Make a choice and defend it.
3. **Ignoring the data**: If the case provides financial data, use it. Quantify your arguments.
4. **Only one alternative considered**: Good analysis requires comparing options, not just arguing for one.
5. **Impractical recommendations**: "The company should become a monopoly" is not actionable.
