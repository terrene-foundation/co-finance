# Research Methods

Practical guidance on conducting finance research -- from evaluating whether a source is credible, to collecting data from major financial databases, to applying basic econometric techniques, to synthesizing findings from multiple studies. Designed for UG/PG students undertaking research projects, honours theses, or dissertations in finance.

## Quick Reference

1. **CRAAP Test**: Currency, Relevance, Authority, Accuracy, Purpose -- five criteria for evaluating sources
2. **Key Databases**: FRED (macro/rates), CRSP (US stock returns), Compustat (fundamentals), WRDS (aggregator)
3. **OLS Assumptions**: Linearity, no perfect multicollinearity, exogeneity, homoscedasticity, no autocorrelation, normality of errors
4. **Event Study**: Measure abnormal returns = actual return - expected return around an event date
5. **Thematic Synthesis**: Organize literature by themes/concepts, not by author or chronology
