# International Finance

Covers the monetary and financial dimensions of international economics -- how exchange rates are determined, how countries account for cross-border flows, the theoretical parity conditions linking prices, interest rates, and exchange rates, and the institutional architecture of the global monetary system. Designed for UG/PG students studying international finance, international economics, or global financial markets.

## Quick Reference

1. **Covered Interest Rate Parity**: F/S = (1 + r_d) / (1 + r_f) -- links forward rates to interest rate differentials
2. **Purchasing Power Parity**: S_1/S_0 = (1 + pi_d) / (1 + pi_f) -- links exchange rate changes to inflation differentials
3. **BOP Identity**: Current Account + Capital Account + Financial Account + Errors = 0
4. **Real Exchange Rate**: q = S x (P_f / P_d) -- adjusts the nominal rate for relative price levels
5. **Impossible Trinity**: A country cannot simultaneously have a fixed exchange rate, free capital flows, and independent monetary policy
