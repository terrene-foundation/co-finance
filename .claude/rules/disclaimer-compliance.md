# Disclaimer & Compliance Guidelines for Academic Writing

## Scope

These rules apply to all academic work that presents financial performance data, investment analysis, portfolio results, or strategy recommendations — including research papers, thesis chapters, case study reports, presentations, and coursework.

## MUST Rules

### 1. Include "Not Financial Advice" Disclaimer

Any academic paper, presentation, or report showing returns, performance data, or investment recommendations MUST include a disclaimer clarifying the educational nature of the work.

**Standard Disclaimer Template** (for papers and reports):

```
DISCLAIMER: This paper is prepared for educational and academic purposes only and does
not constitute financial advice, investment advice, or a recommendation to buy, sell, or
hold any security. Past performance is not indicative of future results. The analysis
presented reflects the author's academic work and should not be used as the basis for
investment decisions.
```

**Short Disclaimer Template** (for presentations and charts):

```
For academic purposes only. Not financial advice. Past performance does not guarantee future results.
```

**Where to place it**: Include the disclaimer on the title page, abstract, or first page of any paper that discusses specific investments, strategies, or performance results. For presentations, include it on the opening or closing slide.

**Enforced by**: peer-reviewer
**Violation**: Must fix before submission

### 2. Mark Hypothetical and Backtested Results Clearly

Any analysis showing backtested, simulated, or hypothetical performance MUST be clearly labeled as such. This is especially important in portfolio construction assignments and strategy analysis papers.

**Required Label** (include near the results):

> HYPOTHETICAL PERFORMANCE: The results presented are based on backtested/simulated performance and have inherent limitations. No representation is made that any portfolio will or is likely to achieve profits or losses similar to those shown. There are frequently material differences between hypothetical performance results and actual results achieved by any investment strategy.

**Short Label** (for tables, charts, and slides):

> Note: Hypothetical/backtested results. These do not represent actual trading. See methodology section for details.

**Enforced by**: peer-reviewer
**Violation**: Must fix before submission

### 3. Include Data Source Attribution

All financial data presented in academic work MUST include the data source, the date range of the data, and (where relevant) the date of retrieval.

**Correct**:

> "Daily adjusted closing prices for the S&P 500 constituents were obtained from the Center for Research in Security Prices (CRSP) via WRDS for the period January 2015 through December 2024, accessed on February 10, 2026."

> "Figure 3: 10-year U.S. Treasury yield, monthly, 2000-2025. Source: Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED), Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis. Retrieved March 1, 2026."

**Incorrect**:

> "We used stock price data for our analysis." (From where? What date range? When was it accessed?)

**Enforced by**: peer-reviewer, citation-specialist
**Violation**: HIGH priority fix

### 4. Use Past Tense for Historical Performance

All references to historical performance MUST use past tense to avoid implying future expectations.

**Correct**:

- "The portfolio returned 12.5% over the sample period."
- "This strategy generated an annualized Sharpe ratio of 1.4 during the backtest period (2015-2024)."
- "The S&P 500 gained 24% in 2023."

**Incorrect**:

- "This portfolio returns 12.5% per year." (Implies it will continue to do so.)
- "This strategy generates a Sharpe ratio of 1.4." (Present tense implies ongoing performance.)
- "The S&P 500 gains 24%." (Ambiguous — past or future?)

**Enforced by**: peer-reviewer
**Violation**: HIGH priority fix

### 5. Include Risk Disclosures with Investment Analysis

Any academic work discussing specific investments, strategies, or asset classes MUST include relevant risk disclosures, even in a student context. This is both good academic practice and a habit that prepares students for professional standards.

**General Risk Disclosure**:

> All investments carry risk, including the potential loss of principal. The value of investments can go down as well as up. Diversification does not guarantee profit or protect against loss in declining markets.

**Strategy-Specific Disclosures** (include as relevant to your analysis):

- **Leverage**: "Leveraged strategies amplify both gains and losses and may result in losses exceeding the original investment."
- **Options**: "Options involve significant risk and can expire worthless, resulting in total loss of the premium paid."
- **International investments**: "International investments involve additional risks including currency fluctuation, political instability, and differing accounting standards."
- **Concentrated portfolios**: "Concentrated portfolios carry higher risk than diversified portfolios. A decline in a single holding may significantly impact overall performance."
- **Cryptocurrency**: "Cryptocurrency investments are highly volatile, largely unregulated, and may result in total loss of investment."

**Enforced by**: peer-reviewer
**Violation**: HIGH priority fix

## MUST NOT Rules

### 1. No Promises of Future Returns

MUST NOT use language that implies or guarantees future investment performance.

**Incorrect**:

- "This strategy will return 15% annually."
- "Investors can expect to earn..."
- "This approach guarantees consistent returns."
- "This portfolio will outperform the benchmark."

**Correct**:

- "During the backtested period (2015-2024), this strategy returned an annualized 15%. Past performance is not indicative of future results."

### 2. No Backtested Results Presented as Actual Performance

MUST NOT present simulated, backtested, or hypothetical results without clearly labeling them as such.

**Incorrect**:

- "Our strategy achieved a 25% return." (Was this real trading or a backtest?)
- "Portfolio performance: +18.3%." (Actual or simulated?)

**Correct**:

- "BACKTESTED RESULT: The strategy achieved a hypothetical 25% return during the simulation period (January 2020 - December 2024). See Section 4 for methodology notes and limitations."

### 3. No Investment Analysis Without Risk Disclosures

MUST NOT present portfolio recommendations, asset allocations, or strategy comparisons without accompanying risk disclosures.

## Disclaimer Placement Guidelines

- **Research papers and thesis chapters**: Full disclaimer on the title page or in a footnote on the first page
- **Presentations**: Short disclaimer on the title slide and/or final slide
- **Charts and tables**: Short disclaimer or source note below every chart showing performance data
- **Executive summaries**: Full disclaimer within the summary if it contains performance figures
- **Problem sets and coursework**: Short disclaimer if the assignment involves recommending specific investments

## Exceptions

Disclaimer exceptions may apply when:

1. The work is purely theoretical with no reference to specific securities or performance data
2. The course instructor explicitly waives the requirement
3. The analysis uses only anonymized or fictional data clearly labeled as such
